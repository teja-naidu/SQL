# Retail Sales and Customer Behavior Analysis - Insights

## Executive Summary

This project analyzes retail sales transactions and customer behavior data to identify revenue drivers, customer purchasing patterns, shopping preferences, and business growth opportunities.

The analysis covers revenue trends, customer demographics, payment methods, shopping mall performance, and customer segmentation.

---

## Dataset Overview

| Metric             | Value           |
| ------------------ | --------------- |
| Total Transactions | 99,457          |
| Total Customers    | 99,457          |
| Total Revenue      | $251.51 Million |

---

## Revenue Insights

### Top Revenue Generating Categories

| Category   | Revenue  |
| ---------- | -------- |
| Clothing   | $113.99M |
| Shoes      | $66.55M  |
| Technology | $57.86M  |

### Key Findings

* Clothing generated the highest revenue among all categories.
* Shoes and Technology were the second and third highest revenue contributors.
* These three categories account for the majority of total sales revenue.

---

## Shopping Mall Performance

### Top Revenue Generating Malls

| Shopping Mall    | Revenue |
| ---------------- | ------- |
| Mall of Istanbul | $50.87M |
| Kanyon           | $50.55M |
| Metrocity        | $37.30M |

### Key Findings

* Mall of Istanbul generated the highest revenue.
* Kanyon performed nearly equal to Mall of Istanbul.
* These malls represent the strongest retail locations in the dataset.

---

## Customer Insights

### Revenue by Gender

| Gender | Revenue  |
| ------ | -------- |
| Female | $150.21M |
| Male   | $101.30M |

### Key Findings

* Female customers generated significantly more revenue than male customers.
* Female shoppers contributed approximately 60% of total revenue.

---

## Age Group Analysis

| Age Group | Revenue |
| --------- | ------- |
| 56+       | $67.29M |
| 36-45     | $50.13M |
| 46-55     | $48.19M |

### Key Findings

* Customers aged 56+ generated the highest revenue.
* Middle-aged customer groups also contributed significantly to sales.

---

## Shopping Behavior Insights

### Revenue by Payment Method

| Payment Method | Revenue  |
| -------------- | -------- |
| Cash           | $112.83M |
| Credit Card    | $88.08M  |
| Debit Card     | $50.60M  |

### Average Order Value

**$2,528.79**

### Key Findings

* Cash remains the most commonly used payment method.
* Credit cards account for a substantial portion of customer spending.
* The average transaction value indicates relatively high-value purchases.

---

## Customer Segmentation

| Segment      | Customers |
| ------------ | --------- |
| Low Value    | 55,670    |
| Medium Value | 27,791    |
| High Value   | 15,996    |

### Key Findings

* Most customers belong to the Low Value segment.
* A smaller group of High Value customers contributes a significant portion of revenue.
* Customer retention and upselling strategies could increase overall revenue.

---

## Business Recommendations

1. Increase marketing investment in Clothing and Shoes categories.
2. Develop loyalty programs targeting Low Value customers.
3. Create personalized campaigns for female shoppers.
4. Focus expansion efforts on top-performing malls.
5. Encourage digital payments through incentives and rewards.
6. Promote premium products to high-spending customer segments.

---

## Conclusion

The analysis identified Clothing as the leading revenue category, Mall of Istanbul as the strongest retail location, female customers as the primary revenue contributors, and Cash as the most frequently used payment method. Customer segmentation revealed strong opportunities for targeted marketing and loyalty programs to increase future revenue.
